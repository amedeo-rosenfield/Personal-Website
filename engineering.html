Over Terrain Vehicle (OTV)

University of Maryland

What?

Led the navigation development of an autonomous over-terrain rescue vehicle, enabling obstacle avoidance, crash site detection, and repair initiation. Planned, built, and iteratively refined the vehicle from scratch under tight deadlines, coordinating full system integration and a final live demonstration.

Click to learn more

How?

  • Programmed the navigation system using C++ in Arduino, building modular movement functions and logic loops to allow autonomous course correction, obstacle avoidance, and waypoint execution.
  • Integrated a Wi-Fi module for real-time communication and telemetry, enabling on-the-fly adjustments during field testing.
  • Conducted regular test runs to iteratively improve navigation accuracy and timing across varied terrains.

Results?

  • While mechanical issues prevented full site repair, the vehicle successfully navigated under bridges, around large obstacles, and into the crash site zone.
  • The navigation system performed reliably across trials, completing the mission course with a 95% success rate.
  • Successfully cut overall navigation time by over 5 minutes, significantly improving mission efficiency and responsiveness.

Anti-Nitrate Water Vessel

Northrop Grumman Innovation Lab

What?

Designed an autonomous vessel to track nitrate pollution entering the Chesapeake Bay to support preservation efforts. Aimed to detect and follow areas of high nitrate concentration using onboard sensors while serving on the sensor calibration team to ensure accurate environmental readings.

Click to learn more

What?

  • Designed an autonomous vessel to track nitrate pollution entering the Chesapeake Bay to support preservation efforts
  • Aimed to detect and follow areas of high nitrate concentration using onboard sensors
  • Served on the sensor calibration team to ensure accurate environmental readings

How?

  • Calibrated turbidity (TBD) and total dissolved solids (TDS) sensors using Arduino (C++)
  • Integrating multiple sensors through a shared microcontroller
  • Enabled real-time data transmission via Wi-Fi and onboard SD card logging

Results?

  • Achieved 90% accuracy with turbidity sensor readings
  • Maintained 1 Hz real-time sampling with 100% logging over 10+ hours
  • Expanding vessel capabilities to detect methane, petroleum, and industrial chemicals, while also improving accuracy
