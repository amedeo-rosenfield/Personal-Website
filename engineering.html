High Speed Train Connector

Glenair Inc.

What?

Engineered a weather-resistant electrical connector for high-speed rail, ensuring durability against rain, wind, and dust while meeting strict size and contact requirements specified by the customer.

How?

  • Designed and assembled 3D connector components using SolidWorks.
  • Produced detailed 2D technical drawings to support precision manufacturing.
  • Collaborated with in-house manufacturers, electricians, and assemblers to finalize the product.
  • Communicated exclusively in Italian during team discussions and daily tasks.

Results?

  • Successfully underwent water, heat, dust, torque, and force testing due to neoprene material used insulating the connectors.
  • Made the assembly process smoother by tweaking design details based on direct input from technicians and production staff.
  • Reduced raw material usage by 15% through thoughtful design optimizations including wall thickness, internal geometry, and contact layout balancing performance with cost and manufacturability.

Over Terrain Vehicle (OTV) Project

University of Maryland

What?

Led the navigation development of an autonomous over-terrain rescue vehicle, enabling obstacle avoidance, crash site detection, and repair initiation. Planned, built, and iteratively refined the vehicle from scratch under tight deadlines, coordinating full system integration and a final live demonstration.

How?

  • Programmed the navigation system using C++ in Arduino, building modular movement functions and logic loops to allow autonomous course correction, obstacle avoidance, and waypoint execution.
  • Integrated a Wi-Fi module for real-time communication and telemetry, enabling on-the-fly adjustments during field testing.
  • Conducted regular test runs to iteratively improve navigation accuracy and timing across varied terrains.

Results?

  • While mechanical issues prevented full site repair, the vehicle successfully navigated under bridges, around large obstacles, and into the crash site zone.
  • The navigation system performed reliably across trials, completing the mission course with a 95% success rate.
  • Successfully cut overall navigation time by over 5 minutes, significantly improving mission efficiency and responsiveness.
